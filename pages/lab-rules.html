Rules

To ensure a safe and productive environment in the Fabrication Lab, please adhere to the following rules:

General Safety Rules

Always Wear PPE:

  • Safety glasses marked Z87+ must be worn at all times.
  • Hearing protection is required when the CNC Router is running.
  • Use gloves when handling sharp objects or chemicals as specified.

Proper Attire:

  • No loose or dangling hair, clothing, or jewelry.
  • Wear long pants and closed-toed shoes with good grip and stability.

No Food or Drink:

  • Unsealed or uncovered food and drinks are not allowed in the lab.

Additive/Subtractive Labs

Authorized Access:

  • Only trained and authorized users may operate lab equipment.
  • Do not attempt to use equipment you are not trained on.

Machine Operation:

  • Follow all operating procedures and guidelines for each machine.
  • Do not leave machines unattended while in operation.
  • Report any malfunctioning equipment to the lab staff immediately.

File Submission:

  • Submit 3D printing files through the designated SharePoint Link
  • Follow the file naming format: firstandlastname_printstyle_materialcolor_classnumber.
  • Ensure files are properly prepared and meet lab specifications before submission.

Usage Etiquette

Time Management:

  • Be mindful of the time you spend on machines; others may be waiting.
  • Book equipment in advance if required, and arrive on time for your slot.

Respect Shared Resources:

  • Do not monopolize equipment or resources.
  • Share tools and materials and return them to their proper place after use.
  • Do not take anything that does not belong to you outside the lab. This includes unpaid prints.
  • 3D prints and milled work are property of the lab until paid for.
  • Do not put bags, drinks, or food on machines. If you cannot find an area, ask a worker for guidance.

Lab Access:

  • Use the lab during designated hours only.
  • Do not bring unauthorized persons into the lab.

Communication and Reporting

Issues and Feedback:

  • Report any issues or concerns to lab staff promptly.
  • Provide feedback to help improve lab operations and services.

Equipment and Machinery Use

Machine Guarding:

  • Always use machine guards provided to protect against moving parts.

Ventilation:

  • Ensure proper ventilation when operating machines or handling chemicals.

Chemical Safety:

  • Always check containers for warnings and read the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for detailed information.
  • Use disposable safety gloves and other PPE as required.

Report Hazards:

  • Immediately report any hazardous conditions or equipment malfunctions to lab staff.

Handling Materials

Sharp Objects:

  • Use cut-resistant gloves when handling sharp objects or during cutting procedures.

Heavy Items:

  • Ask for assistance when lifting heavy items. Use a two-person lift or a hoist if necessary.

Hot Items:

  • Use heat-resistant PPE when handling recently processed parts or machines that have been in use.

Housekeeping

Maintain Clean Workspaces:

  • Keep work areas clean and free of debris to prevent slip and trip hazards.

Dispose of Waste Properly:

  • Dispose of chemical waste and sharp objects in designated containers.

Address Spills Immediately:

  • Clean up any spills immediately and report them to lab staff for further assistance if needed.

Emergency Procedures

Know Emergency Exits:

  • Familiarize yourself with the location of emergency exits and evacuation routes.

Use Fire Extinguishers:

  • Know the location of fire extinguishers and how to use them.

First Aid:

  • Be aware of the location of first aid kits and how to use them in case of an emergency.

By following these rules, you help ensure a safe and efficient working environment for everyone in the Fabrication Lab. Always prioritize safety and ask for assistance if you are unsure about any procedures. For any questions or concerns, please contact the appropriate LSU departments listed in the Emergency Contact Information section.

Stay safe and happy fabricating!
